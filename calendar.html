Event Types

Full Weekend Events

Our Big Events are our Weekend Events. These run from Friday evening (usually starting around 9pm) until Sunday midday (usually 12pm) approximately every other month. Occasionally, we'll run "3-day" events that either start Thursday (9pm) and go through Sunday (12pm) or start Friday (9pm) and go through Monday (12pm). Our schedules are usually posted several months in advance, but COVID has made scheduling a challenge. Look at our announcements on our Social Media Pages for updates on our next events!

These events consist of very interesting combats, role-playing events and more that will further our plot lines of our overall story arc as well as any plots that the PCs present to us.

"Gamedays": Single Day Events

On occasion, we'll run single day events called Gamedays. These Gamedays will last from around 12pm to around 5pm or something like that. Gamedays are rarer now, because most of the sites we've used in the past have been local parks, and that makes for some awkward gaming. We may have more Gamedays forthcoming as we stretch our "wings" again in a less-intense COVID world. Check out our Social Media Pages to find out more about upcoming Gamedays!

Mod Days

We are starting a new program of running some 1-LP events that are called "Mod Days." These Mod Days will consist of approximately 1 large mod of combat with some role playing on either side. Some may also include some OOG hangout time, and maybe even potluck meals. We're still working on the details of this, but we should have more information soon.

Online Events

During our off-season (which is usually in the summer), and in COVID times, we've been exploring online events. These events, which may contain information important to plotlines, or may just be for fun, are advertised on our Social Media Pages, and take place regularly. We even have regular "Tavern Nights" where NPCs just come out and talk to our PCs. Experience may be handed out for some of these events, but that is up to the Refuge Board of Directors.
